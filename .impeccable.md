## Design Context

### Users
FinSight is for everyday people with bank accounts, from first-time budgeters to power users who want deeper control. Primary usage context is desktop (with mobile as a secondary surface) for quick daily check-ins, monthly planning, and transaction review. Core jobs: understand current financial state, track spending patterns, plan goals, and act confidently without spreadsheet-level complexity.

### Brand Personality
Calm, trustworthy, and quietly intelligent. The interface should reduce anxiety, avoid moralizing, and feel like a private instrument rather than an engagement-driven fintech app. Emotion goals: confidence, clarity, and control.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first, premium surfaces with a bright lime accent as a signal color. Strong hierarchy, restrained motion, and dense-but-readable information design inspired by crafted productivity tools (Linear/Raycast-level intentionality) rather than generic SaaS dashboards. Explicit anti-direction: no noisy gamification, no bright consumer-fintech look, no chat-first AI framing.

### Design Principles
1. Surface signal over noise: prioritize decisive, context-rich information instead of decorative metrics.
2. Trust through restraint: emphasis appears only for genuinely important moments.
3. Privacy by design: visuals should feel safe for sensitive financial data and shoulder-surfing contexts.
4. Progressive depth: beginners get plain-language clarity first; advanced controls live one layer deeper.
5. Agentic but quiet: AI assists in the background and appears as actionable insight, not as the visual centerpiece.
